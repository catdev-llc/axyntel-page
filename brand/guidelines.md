## 1. Brand Overview

### 1.1 Mission

Axyntel exists to eliminate guesswork from B2B decisions. We deliver research intelligence that is structured, evidence-based, and actionable -- enabling teams to move faster with greater confidence.

### 1.2 Vision

To become the default research layer for every consequential B2B decision -- from market entry to vendor selection to competitive strategy.

### 1.3 Brand Promise

Every deliverable we produce is grounded in primary sources, structured for clarity, and designed to compress weeks of research into hours.

### 1.4 Brand Personality

| Trait | Description |
|---|---|
| **Authoritative** | We speak from evidence, not opinion. |
| **Precise** | Every claim is specific and quantifiable. |
| **Efficient** | We respect the reader's time. No padding. |
| **Accessible** | Expert-level thinking, presented clearly. |

### 1.5 Brand Voice

The Axyntel voice is that of a senior analyst presenting to a board of directors. Confident without arrogance. Thorough without being verbose. Every sentence earns its place.

**Core principles:**

- Confident and direct
- Precise and evidence-driven
- No fluff, no hype, no filler
- Authoritative but accessible

### 1.6 Tagline

> **"Research Intelligence for B2B Decisions"**

Use the tagline in marketing contexts, below the logo on cover pages, and in email signatures. Never modify or paraphrase it.

### 1.7 Legal Entities

- **CATDEV LLC** -- Registered in Florida, United States
- **CATDEV OU** -- Registered in Estonia, European Union
- **Domain**: axyntel.com

## 2. Logo

### 2.1 Primary Logo

The Axyntel logo is a wordmark: **axyntel.** -- entirely lowercase, terminated by a period in Electric Blue (#3B82F6). The period is a deliberate design element representing precision and finality.

```
axyntel.
^^^^^^^^ Fraunces Semibold (600), lowercase
       ^ Electric Blue period (#3B82F6)
```

### 2.2 Logo Font

- **Typeface**: Fraunces
- **Weight**: SemiBold (600)
- **Style**: Lowercase only
- **Period color**: Electric Blue (#3B82F6)

### 2.3 Minimum Size

- **Digital**: 80px wide minimum
- **Print**: 20mm wide minimum
- Never render the logo smaller than these minimums. Legibility is non-negotiable.

### 2.4 Clear Space

Maintain clear space around the logo equal to the height of the letter "x" in the wordmark on all four sides. No other elements, text, or graphics may enter this zone.

```
   ___________________________
  |                           |
  |   [x]                    |
  |   [x]  axyntel.  [x]    |
  |   [x]                    |
  |                           |
   ---------------------------

  [x] = height of the letter "x"
```

### 2.5 Preferred Usage

| Context | Logo Color | Background |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary (preferred)** | Snow (#F1F5F9) text, Electric Blue period | Midnight (#0A0F1E) |
| **On dark surfaces** | Snow (#F1F5F9) text, Electric Blue period | Navy (#111832) |
| **On light surfaces** | Midnight (#0A0F1E) text, Electric Blue period | White (#FFFFFF) |
| **Monochrome (print)** | Black text and period | White |

### 2.6 Logo Misuse

Do NOT:

- Stretch, skew, or rotate the logo
- Change the typeface
- Recolor the period to anything other than Electric Blue (except monochrome contexts)
- Add drop shadows, outlines, gradients, or glow effects
- Place the logo on busy or low-contrast backgrounds
- Alter the letter spacing
- Capitalize any letters
- Remove the period
- Animate the logo without brand team approval

- All text-on-background combinations must meet WCAG 2.2 AA contrast minimums (4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text).

- Body Text (#1E293B) on White (#FFFFFF) achieves a contrast ratio of approximately 13.2:1.
- Electric Blue (#3B82F6) on Midnight (#0A0F1E) achieves approximately 5.2:1 -- acceptable for large text and UI components, but avoid for small body copy.

## 4. Typography

### 4.1 Type Families

#### Fraunces (Display / Headlines)

- **Source**: Google Fonts
- **Classification**: Old-style serif with variable optical sizing
- **Weights used**: SemiBold (600), Bold (700)
- **Purpose**: Headlines, the logo wordmark, report titles, section headers

#### Inter (Body / UI)

- **Source**: Google Fonts
- **Classification**: Geometric sans-serif
- **Weights used**: Regular (400), Medium (500), SemiBold (600)
- **Purpose**: Body text, UI elements, navigation, captions, buttons

#### JetBrains Mono (Data / Code)

- **Source**: JetBrains
- **Classification**: Monospace
- **Weights used**: Regular (400), Medium (500)
- **Purpose**: Code snippets, data tables, technical identifiers, metrics

### 4.2 Type Scale

| **H2** | Fraunces | 600 | 28px / 1.75rem | 1.3 | -0.01em |
| **H3** | Inter | 600 | 22px / 1.375rem | 1.4 | -0.005em |
| **H4** | Inter | 600 | 18px / 1.125rem | 1.4 | 0 |
| **Body Large** | Inter | 400 | 18px / 1.125rem | 1.6 | 0 |
| **Body** | Inter | 400 | 16px / 1rem | 1.6 | 0 |
| **Body Small** | Inter | 400 | 14px / 0.875rem | 1.5 | 0 |
| **Caption** | Inter | 500 | 12px / 0.75rem | 1.4 | 0.02em |

| **Code** | JetBrains Mono | 400 | 14px / 0.875rem | 1.6 | 0 |

### 4.3 Report Typography

For PDF reports and printed materials, adjust sizes for the print medium:

| Element | Font | Weight | Size | Line Height |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Report Title** | Fraunces | 700 | 32pt | 1.2 |
| **Section Heading** | Fraunces | 600 | 20pt | 1.3 |
| **Subsection** | Inter | 600 | 14pt | 1.4 |
| **Body** | Inter | 400 | 11pt | 1.6 |
| **Table Data** | Inter | 400 | 10pt | 1.4 |
| **Footnote** | Inter | 400 | 8pt | 1.4 |

### 4.4 Typography Rules

- Never use more than two type families on a single page (Fraunces + Inter, or Inter + JetBrains Mono).
- Headlines always use Fraunces. Body always uses Inter. Data always uses JetBrains Mono.
- Maintain consistent vertical rhythm using a 4px baseline grid.
- Paragraphs: use 1em (16px) spacing between paragraphs, not indentation.
- Maximum line length: 65-75 characters for body text to ensure readability.

## 5. Iconography

### 5.1 Icon Style

Axyntel uses outline-style (stroke) icons exclusively. Filled icons are not part of the brand vocabulary.

- **Stroke weight**: 2px
- **Line caps**: Round
- **Line joins**: Round
- **Corner radius**: Proportional to icon size

### 5.2 Icon Sizes

| Size | Usage |
|---|---|
| **16px** | Inline with body text, table indicators, compact UI |
| **20px** | Navigation items, list icons, form field icons |
| **24px** | Section headers, primary actions, standalone icons |

### 5.3 Icon Colors

Icons inherit color from their context:

- **Electric Blue (#3B82F6)**: Highlighted or active icons
- **Steel (#64748B)**: Neutral or inactive icons
- **Snow (#F1F5F9)**: Icons on dark backgrounds
- **Body Text (#1E293B)**: Icons in report/light contexts

### 5.4 Icon Library

Preferred source: Lucide Icons (lucide.dev). Lucide provides the consistent stroke style, 2px weight, and rounded aesthetic that aligns with the Axyntel brand.

## 6. Imagery and Photography

### 6.1 Guiding Principles

Axyntel's visual language is rooted in data, structure, and clarity. Imagery should feel analytical, not decorative.

### 6.2 Preferred Imagery

- **Abstract data visualizations**: Network graphs, node maps, structured grids
- **Geometric patterns**: Subtle, derived from data structures
- **Gradient glows**: Soft Electric Blue or Electric Light glows on dark backgrounds
- **Charts and diagrams**: Clean, minimal, using the brand color palette

### 6.3 Background Treatments

- Dark backgrounds (Midnight or Navy) with subtle radial gradient glows
- Gradient direction: from Electric Blue (center/top) fading into Midnight
- Noise texture: optional, at 2-4% opacity for subtle depth
- Never use solid flat backgrounds on marketing materials; always add subtle dimensionality

### 6.4 What to Avoid

- Stock photography of people shaking hands, pointing at screens, or generic office scenes
- Clipart or cartoon illustrations
- Generic AI-generated imagery (glowing brains, robot hands, etc.)
- Overly saturated or neon color treatments
- Busy or cluttered compositions
- Low-resolution or pixelated assets

## 8. Voice and Tone Guidelines

### 8.1 Writing Principles

| Principle | Description |
|---|---|
| **Be direct** | Lead with the conclusion. Support with evidence. |
| **Be precise** | Quantify claims. Cite sources. Avoid generalizations. |
| **Be efficient** | Every sentence must earn its place. Cut ruthlessly. |
| **Be honest** | Acknowledge limitations. Flag uncertainty. Never overstate. |

### 8.2 Do

- Use active voice
- Cite evidence and sources
- Use precise language and specific numbers
- Quantify claims ("34% of respondents" not "many respondents")
- Write short paragraphs (3-4 sentences maximum)
- Use bullet points for lists of three or more items
- Define acronyms on first use

### 8.3 Do Not

- Use buzzwords ("synergy," "leverage," "paradigm shift," "disruptive")
- Make vague promises ("best-in-class," "world-class," "cutting-edge")
- Use exclamation marks excessively (one per page maximum, and only when genuinely warranted)
- Write in passive voice unless the agent is deliberately unknown
- Use filler phrases ("it goes without saying," "at the end of the day," "in order to")
- Hedge excessively ("we think maybe possibly this could potentially")

### 8.4 Examples

#### Website Copy

**Bad**: "We leverage cutting-edge AI to deliver world-class research solutions that will revolutionize your business!"

**Good**: "Axyntel structures primary-source research into decision-ready intelligence. Clients report compressing 3-week research cycles into 48 hours."

#### Email Subject Lines

**Bad**: "Exciting News! Check Out Our Amazing New Platform!!!!"

**Good**: "Your market intelligence report is ready -- 47 sources analyzed"

#### Report Headers

**Bad**: "A Comprehensive and In-Depth Analysis of the Revolutionary Cloud Computing Market Landscape and Its Incredible Opportunities"

**Good**: "European Cloud Infrastructure Market: Competitive Landscape and Entry Opportunities, Q1 2026"

#### LinkedIn Posts

**Bad**: "Thrilled to announce our AMAZING new feature! We are SO excited about this game-changing innovation that will disrupt the industry!! Like and share!!"

**Good**: "We analyzed 2,400 vendor evaluation processes across 12 industries. The finding: 73% of B2B buyers make shortlist decisions before speaking to sales. The research that shapes that shortlist is where the leverage is. Full breakdown: axyntel.com/report"

#### Microcopy (Buttons, Labels)

**Bad**: "Click Here!" / "Submit Your Information"

**Good**: "View Report" / "Start Research"

## 9. Report Template Standards

### 9.1 Title Page

- **Background**: Midnight (#0A0F1E), full bleed
- **Logo**: axyntel. wordmark, centered, upper third
- **Title**: Fraunces Bold (700), 32pt, Snow (#F1F5F9), centered
- **Subtitle/Client**: Inter Medium (500), 14pt, Silver (#CBD5E1), centered below title
- **Date**: Inter Regular (400), 12pt, Steel (#64748B), centered
- **Confidential notice**: Inter Regular (400), 10pt, Steel (#64748B), bottom center
- **Accent**: A subtle horizontal line in Electric Blue (#3B82F6), 2px, 80px wide, between logo and title

### 9.2 Section Headers

- **H1**: Fraunces SemiBold (600), 20pt, Heading Text (#0F172A)
- **Accent**: A 3px horizontal line in Electric Blue (#3B82F6), spanning the full content width, immediately below the H1. 8pt spacing above and below the line.
- **H2**: Inter SemiBold (600), 14pt, Heading Text (#0F172A)
- **H3**: Inter SemiBold (600), 12pt, Body Text (#1E293B)

### 9.3 Body Content

- **Background**: White (#FFFFFF)
- **Text**: Body Text (#1E293B), Inter Regular (400), 11pt
- **Margins**: 1 inch (25.4mm) on all sides; 1.25 inches on left for binding when printed
- **Paragraph spacing**: 6pt after each paragraph
- **Maximum width**: Content should not exceed 6.5 inches (165mm) on a US Letter page

### 9.4 Citations and Footnotes

- **In-text**: Superscript numbers [1], [2], etc.
- **Footnote format**: [Number] Author/Source. "Title." Publication, Date. URL (if applicable).
- **Footnote font**: Inter Regular (400), 8pt, Steel (#64748B)
- **Footnote placement**: Bottom of the page, separated by a 1px Slate rule

Example:

> [1] Gartner. "Market Guide for AI-Augmented Development." Gartner Research, March 2026. https://gartner.com/doc/example

### 9.5 Tables in Reports

- **Header**: Navy (#111832) background, White text, Inter SemiBold (600), 10pt
- **Rows**: Alternating White / Subtle Background (#F8FAFC)
- **Borders**: 1px solid #E2E8F0
- **Numeric data**: Right-aligned, JetBrains Mono Regular (400), 10pt
- **Text data**: Left-aligned, Inter Regular (400), 10pt

### 9.6 Page Numbers

- **Position**: Bottom right of every page except the title page
- **Font**: Inter Regular (400), 9pt, Steel (#64748B)
- **Format**: "Page X of Y"

### 9.7 Headers and Footers

- **Header (pages 2+)**: Report title in Inter Regular (400), 9pt, Steel (#64748B), top left. Axyntel logo mark or "axyntel." in 9pt, top right.
- **Footer**: Page number bottom right. Optional confidentiality notice bottom left in 8pt Steel.

## 10. Application Examples

### 10.1 Website (Dark Theme)

The Axyntel website uses the dark palette as its foundation:

- **Background**: Midnight (#0A0F1E)
- **Navigation**: Navy (#111832) with Slate border-bottom
- **Cards**: Navy background, Slate borders, 16px border radius
- **Headings**: Fraunces, Snow (#F1F5F9)
- **Body text**: Inter, Silver (#CBD5E1)
- **CTAs**: Electric Blue buttons
- **Links**: Electric Light (#60A5FA), no underline; underline on hover
- **Footer**: Midnight background, Steel text

### 10.2 Reports and PDFs (Light Theme with Dark Title Page)

- **Title page**: Full dark treatment (see Section 9.1)
- **Interior pages**: White background, dark text
- **Accent usage**: Electric Blue for horizontal rules, key metrics, and chart highlights
- **Charts**: Use the full brand palette; Electric Blue for primary data series, Steel for secondary

### 10.3 Email Signatures

```
[Name]
[Title] | Axyntel
[email] | axyntel.com

Research Intelligence for B2B Decisions
```

- **Name**: Inter SemiBold (600), Body Text color
- **Details**: Inter Regular (400), Steel color
- **Tagline**: Inter Regular (400), italic, Steel color
- No images, no logos in email signatures (to avoid rendering issues across clients)

### 10.4 LinkedIn Posts

- **Tone**: Analytical, data-forward, insight-led
- **Structure**: Hook (1-2 sentences with a specific number or finding) followed by context followed by a link
- **Formatting**: Use line breaks for readability. No emoji walls. One relevant emoji per post maximum, if any.
- **Hashtags**: Maximum of 3, placed at the end. Preferred: #B2BResearch #MarketIntelligence #Axyntel

### 10.5 Presentation Slides

- **Background**: Midnight (#0A0F1E)
- **Title slides**: Fraunces Bold, Snow, centered
- **Content slides**: Left-aligned, Inter for body, generous whitespace
- **Data slides**: Charts and tables following the brand color palette
- **Accent**: Electric Blue for emphasis, underlines, and chart highlights
- **Avoid**: Transitions, animations, clip art, decorative elements
